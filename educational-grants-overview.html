Educational Grants Overview

By wpadmin on February 12, 2019
  • Share
  • Share
  • Share
  • 0 Comments

Do you need help paying for your college education? Whether youre working towards an associate, undergraduate, or graduate degree, a grant may help you pay for a portion of your education. These non-repayable funds are a form of student financial aid that can assist you in paying for your tuition, room, board, and even textbooks. As with scholarships, you will not be responsible for paying back any of the grant money you qualify for. Some of the most commonly recognized grants include the Federal Pell Grant and theFederal Supplemental Education Opportunity Grant.

Difference Between a Grant and a Scholarship

Grants and scholarships are often confused because neither require that you pay back the money awarded. Its for this reason that both are often referred to as gift aid. Grants and scholarships generally have set requirements that must be met by recipients to continue receiving funding, such as GPA expectations or an educational timeframe.There is, however, a major difference between these two financial aid options. While scholarships are generally awarded based on merit, grants are most frequently provided based on need. The amount of money you can receive from a grant is usually determined by your and your familys total yearly income.

Grant Types

There are several different grant types you should be aware of. The most common grant types include federal grants, college-based grants, and private grants.

Federal Grants

Federal grants are funded by the government and generally provide financial aid that ranges from $100 to $5,000. The four main types of Federal Student Aid provided by the United States Department of Education are:

  • Federal Pell Grant
  • Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant
  • Teacher Education Assistant for College and Higher Education (TEACH) Grant
  • Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant

Its important to note that the TEACH Grant differs from other aid options. This grant requires students to complete certain classes and to graduate with an education degree. If graduates then work for four complete years in a high-need field at an eligible school, the aid is free. However, failing to meet grant requirements will turn the free aid into a loan.

College-Based Grants

Many colleges and universities also offer grants to students. In fact, educational institutions can provide a grant for any reason, including as an incentive to participate in an extracurricular activity or as additional aid to those in low income families.

Private Grants

There are also private grant options. This money is provided by outside organizations and companies. Organizations like the Boy Scouts of America often have grant programs, as well as labor unions and churches.

How to Apply

To qualify for a grant, you will need to fill out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). This application requires recent tax information, so ensure you have this on hand when beginning the process. After youve submitted your FAFSA, the colleges and universities youve applied to will be able to determine how much aid you qualify for. Upon admittance, youll receive a financial aid award letter that indicates which grants you received. Its important to file your FAFSA as early as possible, as grant money is generally awarded on a first-come, first-serve basis.

In most cases, private grants must be applied for separately through the providing organization or company. Some grants are automatically awarded, but most will require completing an application process.

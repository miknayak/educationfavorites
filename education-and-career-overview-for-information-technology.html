Education and Career Overview for Information Technology

Are you interested in pursuing an information technology (IT) career? An IT career is ideal for individuals who enjoy working with computersin any number of different capacities, including communications, software development, and networking. This field offers a wide range of professional opportunities, but some of the most prominent position titles are:

  • Application Analyst
  • Computer Network Architect
  • Computer Programmer
  • Computer Support Specialists
  • Cyber Security Analyst
  • Data Analyst
  • Data Scientist
  • Database Administrator
  • Information and Technology Consultant
  • Information Systems Manger
  • Penetration Tester
  • Software Developer
  • Systems Analyst
  • Systems Developer
  • Web Developer

Degree Options

Because the IT field is so vast, there are professional positions available at every level. While some jobs may require little formal education, most employers give preference to those with some sort of degree. Education options include associate, undergraduate, graduate, and doctorate level degrees.

Associate Degree

An associate degree in IT often provides students with a broad overview of computer science and technology. They are ideal for individuals seeking entry-level jobs or who intend to continue their education in the future. These programs generally consist of about 60 credit hours of coursework and can be completed within two years.

Undergraduate Degree

While it’s possible to find IT work with an associate degree, most employers require candidates to have an undergraduate degree. Bachelor’s degrees provide a basic introduction to the field, as well as an opportunity to delve deeper into specific and more advanced elements. These programs are usually comprised of 120 credit hours of coursework that full-time students usually complete in four years.

Graduate Degree

An undergraduate degree is usually required, but a graduate IT degree is often preferred. This type of program allows for an in-depth exploration of a specific aspect of the field.A graduate degree can range from 30 to 60 credit hours of coursework and often take two years to complete.

Doctoral Degree

Most professionals find that a graduate degree is enough to qualify them for top IT jobs. Those interested in teaching, consulting, and management may, however, benefit from earning a doctorate. Programs generally consist of about 60 credit hours of coursework that students can complete in three to five years.

Fields of Study

IT is a very broad term for a vastly large field. There are a plethora of job opportunities for individuals with an IT degree. Before selecting your program, spend some time researching each of the following fields of study:

  • Computer Programming
  • Computer Information Systems
  • Technology Management
  • Computer Support
  • Wed Development
  • Programming
  • Cybersecurity
  • Networking
  • Management Information Systems
  • Computer Engineering
  • Computer Science

Career and Salary Outlook

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, there will be 13 percent more IT positions available in 2026 than there were in 2016. This growth will account for approximately 557,100 new jobs and is likely due to an increased emphasis on cloud computing, information security, and data storage. The rate of growth in this field is faster than was projected for many other professions.

Additionally, IT professionals make decent money. In 2017, the Bureau of Labor Statistics reported that the median annual wage for computer and information technology occupations was $85,580.This is well above the median annual wage for all other occupations.

Sources:

Understanding University Programs

Are you preparing to start college? Finding the university program that’s right for you can seem like a daunting task. While some people easily identify their program preference, others may require more time. Fortunately, you still have plenty of time to experience new things, learn about potential career options, and explore the world of higher education. In fact, most colleges and universities factor in some level of indecision and make it relatively easy to shift degree paths if necessary.

While you don’t need to make the final decision right now, it is important that you understand the university program options available to you.

Degree Types

Most fields have at least a few entry-level positions that you may be qualified to apply for out of high school, but most employers prefer to see some form of higher education. The level of education needed, however, varies greatly depending on your chosen profession. There are university programs at all education levels, including associate, undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral.

Associate Degree

Associate degrees are ideal for those interested in a field but not ready to commit to a longer program. These degrees generally provide a broad course overview or prepare students for a specific role within an industry. While programs vary, they generally consist of 60 credit hours of coursework. Full-time students can expect to complete them within two years.

Undergraduate Degree

Most employers expect candidates to have, at minimum, an undergraduate degree. There are two undergraduate program types: Bachelor of Arts (BA) and Bachelor of Science (BS). BA degree tend to be broad in scope, including coursework related to the arts and humanities. BS degrees, on the other hand, are specialized and technical. Every program is different, but most consist of 120 credit hours and can be completed by full-time students over a four-year period.

Graduate Degree

Undergraduate degrees are often required, but graduate degrees are usually preferred. These programs are ideal for students interested in a more in-depth exploration of a specific aspect of their field. In most cases, graduate programs are between 30 to 60 credit hours and take full-time students two years to complete.

Doctoral Degree

Most professions don’t require a doctoral degree. In fact, some fields don’t even offer them; those interested in further education must find a related program instead. Individuals who want to pursue research positions will almost always need doctoral degree, as will those who plan to teach at a college level. Consultants and managers can also benefit greatly from a doctorate. Depending on the desired degree, programs can range from 60 to 120 credit hours of coursework and often include some form of dissertation. As personal circumstances and field specifics play a large role in determining the exact program timeframe, students will need to research this carefully. Completion may take as few as three years, or as many as seven.

Popular University Programs

There are numerous university programs to choose from. Exact program titles and offerings may vary by institution and state, but some of the most common options include:

  • History
  • English Language and Literature
  • Accounting
  • Criminal Justice
  • Teacher Education
  • Biology
  • Nursing
  • Psychology
  • Business Administration and Management
